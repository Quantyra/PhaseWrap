# Q-RoPE Braid Failure Boundary Theory Note v1

Date: 2026-03-12
Stories: S957

## Purpose
Explain, at memo level, why the `braid` transfer family is the current archived failure boundary while `path`, `loop-closure`, and `fork-join` remain preserved transfer evidence.

## Standing Contrast
### Preserved Families
- `path`
- `loop-closure`
- `fork-join`

### Archived Family
- `braid`

## Narrow Internal Hypothesis
The preserved transfer families retain a witness advantage when the target can still be expressed through stable latent-conditioned relational responses over a bounded perturbation family. The `braid` family failed because deeper structural perturbation exposed a representation that the bounded symbolic control could match without requiring hidden latent lookup.

## Why Braid Is Different
### 1. Hardening Shape
- `path`, `loop-closure`, and `fork-join` stayed ahead through their full bounded nuisance and structural cycles.
- `braid` stayed positive on the base packet and shallow hardening, but failed specifically at deeper structural pairing perturbation.

### 2. Likely Structural Interpretation
- The braid construction appears to compress into a smaller effective relational state under deeper pairing perturbation than the other preserved transfer families.
- Once that compression happens, the frozen symbolic basis can recover enough of the useful structure to match or beat the witness.

### 3. Why This Does Not Contradict The Preserved Result
- The benchmark plus preserved transfers do not claim universal transfer across all relational task families.
- The braid failure is valuable because it defines a concrete boundary: some ostensibly richer relational families still collapse under deeper structural reindexing.

## Practical Internal Claim
- The witness result is robust across multiple materially different transfer families.
- It is not robust across every tested transfer family.
- The braid line marks a useful design constraint for future task construction: avoid transfer targets whose relational state can collapse into compact symbolic recovery under deeper pairing perturbation.

## What This Suggests For Future Task Design
- Prefer transfer families where latent-conditioned relational responses remain non-compressible under deeper reindexing.
- Treat deeper structural perturbation as a required decision gate, not an optional extra.
- Use `braid` as the internal counterexample when evaluating whether a new transfer family is genuinely worth implementing.
