# Q-RoPE Transfer Portfolio Structure Theory v1

Date: 2026-03-12

## BLUF
The preserved transfer families appear to share one useful property: their latent-conditioned relational responses remain non-compressible under the bounded nuisance and structural perturbations we used. `braid` is the current counterexample because deeper pairing perturbation appears to compress its effective relational state into something the bounded symbolic family can approximate.

## Preserved Positive Families
- `path`
- `loop-closure`
- `fork-join`
- `relay-binding`

## Archived Negative Family
- `braid`

## Shared Structural Pattern In The Preserved Families
### 1. Latent relay or accumulation is not a superficial wrapper
- `path` accumulates along a directed sequence.
- `loop-closure` adds a closure constraint across the accumulated path.
- `fork-join` requires branch separation plus recombination.
- `relay-binding` requires a delayed bind through an intermediate relay.

These are not just larger feature sets. They require relational state to remain meaningful across at least one intermediate transformation.

### 2. Deeper perturbation did not collapse the witness advantage
For the preserved families, the witness remained ahead after bounded nuisance and structural tests. That means the useful relational signal did not reduce to a compact symbolic summary under the perturbation family we allowed.

### 3. The bounded symbolic controls remained summary-based rather than state-reconstructive
The frozen symbolic families had access to declared summaries and bounded interactions, but not latent path-state identities or hidden tuple lookup. The preserved families still kept a measurable gap under that fairness rule.

## Why `braid` Failed In Contrast
### 1. Braiding appears to compress under deeper reindexing
The `braid` family survived shallow hardening but failed at deeper pairing perturbation. That suggests its relational state can be reorganized into a smaller effective summary under reindexing.

### 2. The symbolic family could recover that compressed structure
Once compressed, the bounded symbolic control no longer needed hidden latent recovery. It could approximate enough of the useful structure from declared summaries alone.

### 3. This identifies a real boundary, not noise
`braid` is not a random failure. It is evidence that some relational task families only appear strong until deeper structural perturbation reveals a symbolic compression path.

## Working Structural Hypothesis
A transfer family is more likely to survive if:
- useful signal depends on delayed relational binding or accumulated path state,
- deeper reindexing does not collapse that state into a small symbolic summary,
- bounded symbolic controls cannot reconstruct the necessary state from declared summaries alone.

A transfer family is more likely to fail if:
- the relational state can be permuted or reindexed into a compact symbolic representation,
- the witness advantage depends mainly on shallow ordering artifacts,
- or the latent structure is effectively recoverable from frozen declared summaries after deeper perturbation.
